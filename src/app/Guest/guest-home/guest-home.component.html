Fresh Honey Since 1990

The main indigenous groups represented are mainly those who have maintained a traditional practice of collecting honey from the wild for over 2000 years. This tradition has not only been a source of food and recently income, but is also deeply incorporated into the cultural and spiritual aspects of the lives of these various indigenous communities.

Alongside the details about honey hunting there will also be information about beekeeping, as it relates to areas or regions that are practicing it. The honey category will discuss the natural components and common uses of honey. And the conservation category will describe the local ecosystems/reserves and the natural support they give, as well as why it is crucial to support them back.
